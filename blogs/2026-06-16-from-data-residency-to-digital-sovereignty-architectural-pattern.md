Digital sovereignty has evolved from a policy discussion into a practical platform engineering concern, with regulations like the EU Data Act and NIS-2 shaping architecture decisions. The piece proposes tenant clusters as a sovereignty primitive, where each tenant cluster has its own API server, controller manager, scheduler, and data store to establish meaningful jurisdictional boundaries.
